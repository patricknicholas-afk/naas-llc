Case Study

Anheuser-Busch

Building a unified design system for one of the world's largest beverage companies — creating consistency across 500+ brands while preserving each brand's distinct identity.

Role Design Systems Lead
Timeline 14 Months
Platform Web & Mobile
Scale 500+ Brands

Overview

AB InBev operates 500+ brands across 150+ markets. Each brand had its own design team, agency relationships, and digital presence — resulting in a fragmented ecosystem where every campaign or digital touchpoint was built from scratch. Marketing teams were spending more time negotiating with agencies about button styles than crafting strategy. I was brought in to lead the design system initiative that would change that.

Deliverables

3-Tier Token System, 45-Component Library, Figma + React, Documentation Platform

Methods

Component Audit, Stakeholder Interviews, Pilot Program, Accessibility Review

Team

Design systems lead, 3 engineers, 25+ brand stakeholders

Outcome

75% faster launches, $2.3M annual savings, 100% WCAG AA compliance

01

Research & Discovery

Component Audit

The fragmentation was worse than anyone realized

I led a comprehensive audit across 40 brand properties — cataloging every unique component, interaction pattern, and design decision. The results were eye-opening: 23 different button styles, 14 navigation patterns, and 8 distinct form implementations doing essentially the same thing.

I interviewed 25+ stakeholders across brand teams, engineering, marketing, and agency partners. The tension was sharp but clarifying:

  • Brand teams feared a design system would flatten their personality into generic templates
  • Engineering wanted shared code but couldn't adopt it if every brand needed custom overrides
  • Marketing needed faster launch times without sacrificing brand authenticity
  • Agencies resisted standardization because bespoke work justified their retainers
  • Leadership wanted consistency and cost reduction without understanding the technical tradeoffs

Component audit across 40 brand properties — cataloging every unique pattern

"Brand differentiation lives in a small number of high-impact properties — color, typography, imagery, tone. Structural patterns should be shared. The system needed to be a platform, not a template."

— Core insight from stakeholder research

02

Design Process

Token Architecture

Three tiers. One source of truth.

I designed a three-tier token system that became the foundation of the entire design system:

  • Tier 1 — Global tokens: Spacing scale, grid, breakpoints, elevation, motion. Shared across every brand, never overridden.
  • Tier 2 — Brand tokens: Color palettes, typography stacks, border radius, imagery guidelines. Each brand defines its own set.
  • Tier 3 — Component tokens: Map semantic roles to brand values. This is where personality meets structural consistency.

A "Button" component could look distinctly Budweiser or distinctly Stella Artois while sharing identical accessibility, interaction, and responsive behavior.

Three-tier token architecture — global → brand → component

Component Library

45 components. Every state covered.

I designed 45 core components with full state coverage — default, hover, active, focused, disabled, loading, error, and empty states. Each component was documented with usage guidelines, do/don't examples, and accessibility requirements.

A design system without governance dies. I established a contribution model with clear criteria for when to extend the system vs. when to create a brand-specific component, a review process for new additions, and quarterly audits to catch drift.

Component states — all 8 interaction states documented across 45 components

The Solution

A new brand can go from zero to a fully themed, accessible component set in under 2 hours.

Brew — the theming engine showing Budweiser, Stella Artois, and a craft label variant side by side

Documentation as Product

Adoption depends on discoverability

The documentation site includes interactive component playgrounds, brand token previews, copy-paste code snippets, and a decision-tree tool for choosing the right component. I designed the docs as a product — because a design system that nobody uses is just a Figma file.

I also designed 12 page templates covering the most common brand needs: product landing pages, campaign microsites, age gates, store locators, and event pages. Pre-wired with analytics and accessibility compliance.

What I Learned

Reflection

The hardest part of a design system isn't the components — it's the politics

Adoption is a change management problem, not a design problem. The token architecture was the technical breakthrough, but the governance model and stakeholder alignment were what made it stick.

The three-tier token architecture became the reference model for AB InBev's broader digital transformation, with the pattern being evaluated for their mobile app portfolio. The best systems don't just solve today's problem — they create the conditions for tomorrow's solution.

3 pilot brands → 12 active brands within 6 months of launch
